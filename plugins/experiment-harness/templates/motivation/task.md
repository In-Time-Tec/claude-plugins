# Task: Overcast — a landing page

Build a one-page marketing site for a fictional, slightly-obscure membership club called **Overcast**. The club is for people who genuinely prefer gloomy weather — the ones who feel most themselves under grey skies, in drizzle, in fog. Members receive a monthly mailed card describing a real, overcast place somewhere in the world, along with a small related object (a pressed fern, a piece of sea glass, a photograph).

## Deliverable

A single directory containing:

- `index.html` — the landing page.
- `styles.css` — all styling.

No JavaScript. No build step. No external frameworks or CDNs (inline the fonts you need or rely on web-safe fonts).

## The page must include

- A hero section naming the club and communicating its premise in under 30 seconds of reading.
- A short section describing what members receive.
- A short "who this is for" section.
- A simple pricing block (one tier is fine; make up a price).
- A sign-up call-to-action (form is fine; it does not need to actually submit anywhere — use `action="#"` or a plain mailto).
- Adequate footer with made-up but plausible details (contact address, copyright year).

## What you have latitude on

- Visual tone, color palette, typography, imagery choices.
- How much content to write in each section.
- Whether to include extras (a testimonials block, an FAQ, etc.).
- Responsive breakpoints and their specifics.

## What is fixed

- The club's name: **Overcast**.
- The premise: a monthly mailed card + small object tied to an overcast place.
- Plain HTML + CSS only (no JS, no frameworks).
- Everything ships from `index.html` and `styles.css` in one directory.

## Constraints you should respect

- Use semantic HTML elements (`<header>`, `<main>`, `<section>`, etc.) where appropriate.
- Every image needs an `alt` attribute (even if the image is decorative and the alt is empty — make that choice deliberately).
- The page should render acceptably at 375px and 1200px widths.
- Content you invent should be plausible and coherent with the premise. No lorem ipsum in the final output.
